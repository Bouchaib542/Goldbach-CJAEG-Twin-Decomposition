Scientific Report: CJAEG Method

1. Principle

The CJAEG method (Conjecture des Jumeaux Anti-Équidistants de Goldbach) proposes that for any even number E ≥ 4, there exists at least one pair of primes (p, q) such that:

  • E = A + B (partition of E into two integers A and B)
  • There exists an integer s such that: p = A − s and q = B + s, or p = A + s and q = B − s
  • At least one of p or q belongs to a twin prime pair (i.e., p ± 2 or q ± 2 is also prime)

This structure is said to be anti-equidistant: the prime numbers are not centered around E/2, but around two separate integers A and B that form a partition of E.

2. Motivation

Unlike traditional methods which often search symmetrically around E/2, CJAEG relies on asymmetric exploration of partitions of E. This can reveal deeper regularities in the distribution of primes—particularly in their tendency to form twin pairs across anti-symmetric positions.

3. Empirical Observations

Extensive tests up to E = 10⁹ have shown that such decompositions exist, and that the shift s needed is typically much smaller than the symmetric t used in GPS-like methods. This suggests a more efficient predictive route.

Example:
For E = 100:
Partition A = 30, B = 70 → with s = 13 → A − s = 17, B + s = 83 → both primes.
And (17, 19) form a twin pair.

4. Current Conjecture

CJAEG Conjecture (Bahbouhi, 2025):
For any even number E ≥ 4, there exists a partition E = A + B and a positive integer s such that:

  • p = A − s and q = B + s are both prime, or
  • p = A + s and q = B − s are both prime
  • And at least one of (p, q) is part of a twin prime pair

5. Implications

This method opens a new path toward understanding the structure of primes involved in Goldbach’s Conjecture. It provides an alternative mechanism—centered on partition dynamics and twin primes—to validate the decomposition of even numbers at massive scales.
