About the CJAEG Conjecture

Author: Bahbouhi Bouchaib
Independent researcher in mathematics
Based in Nantes, France

The CJAEG method (Conjecture des Jumeaux Anti-Équidistants de Goldbach) is a novel mathematical approach to decomposing any even number E into a pair of prime numbers (p, q) such that:

  • E = A + B is a partition of E
  • p = A ± s and q = B ∓ s for some integer s
  • At least one of p or q is part of a twin prime pair

This hypothesis expands the traditional GPS method by using anti-equidistant structures and twin prime configurations to explain the persistent validity of Goldbach's Conjecture for extremely large even numbers.

This site is a public tool intended to support further exploration of the CJAEG conjecture.
